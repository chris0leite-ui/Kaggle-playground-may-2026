## Why all four failed (load-bearing lesson, sharpened by H1)

All four candidates achieved **low ρ vs PRIMARY** (0.917 to 0.965) —
predictions disagree meaningfully with PRIMARY. Yet min-meta lift was
zero or slightly negative across the board. Disagreement without
incremental signal = **noise**, not orthogonal information.

H1 sharpens the lesson by closing the GBDT/FM dichotomy: G1 and G2'
were LGBM bases (same class as 13/21 PRIMARY bases). H1 was a **FM
base** with a **genuinely new 3-way concat field** explicitly chosen
from the EDA's top-3 hypotheses — and it ALSO hit the same noise wall.

> **The PRIMARY pool's meta layer (now hier-meta) has already absorbed
> the signal that any single new base built on existing features /
> existing model classes can encode.** New features inside an existing
> class — LGBM, FM, sparse-LR — don't break the ceiling; they
> redistribute noise.
>
> The remaining EV axes are:
> 1. Genuinely **new model classes** (TabPFN, hazard NN done right) —
>    Move A on main.
> 2. **Meta-layer innovations** beyond Path B (e.g. Compound × Stint,
>    Driver-grouped, multi-level hier-meta).
> 3. **Target reformulation** (predict something else and back out
>    PitNextLap; not yet attempted from the right angle — earlier
>    t12 multi-formulation was T1.2 GBDT class, not target-side).
> 4. **External data** (Pirelli pit-window etc. — Day-12 deprecated
>    on synth-data-doesn't-match grounds, may need re-examination).
