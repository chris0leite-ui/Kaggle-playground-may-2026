## Headline finding

**The 2023 portion of synth has a structurally different `PitNextLap`
label distribution from 2022/2024/2025**: pit rate is 0.96% in 2023
vs 26-30% in other years (a **28-30× difference**). The K=4 PRIMARY
predicts well on 2023 (AUC 0.95054, mean OOF 0.0091) but the 2023
sub-distribution itself is fundamentally different.

| Year | n_train | pos_rate | OOF AUC | mean OOF |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 2022 | 82,989 | 0.2665 | 0.91923 | 0.2667 |
| **2023** | **136,147** | **0.0096** | 0.95054 | 0.0091 |
| 2024 | 127,110 | 0.2953 | 0.93402 | 0.2953 |
| 2025 | 92,894 | 0.2844 | 0.93449 | 0.2850 |

Test set has the same proportional distribution: 35k/58k/55k/40k for
2022/2023/2024/2025. So **31% of LB ROC-AUC is computed on the
near-zero-pit 2023 subset.**

## Hypothesis: aadigupta1601's 2023 portion has different source

Most plausible explanation: aadigupta1601 (the orig) has
heterogeneous source data per year. The 2023 portion is likely from
**practice/qualifying/sprint sessions** (where pit stops are rare,
drivers do hot laps), while 2022/2024/2025 are from **race sessions**
(pit stops mandatory, ~50% of mid-stint laps see pit decisions).

Alternative: 2023's PitNextLap label may be computed differently
(e.g., "was the LAST lap before pit?" instead of "next-lap pit"),
which would compress positive rate.

The host's CTGAN faithfully reproduces this per-year source
heterogeneity, explaining why synth 2023 looks structurally different
from synth 2022/2024/2025.

## Implication for prediction

- **K=4 PRIMARY is well-calibrated per year** (mean_OOF ≈ pos_rate
  per year). No mass calibration error.
- **Public-LB AUC weights all rows uniformly**, so 2023's near-zero
  rates contribute mostly to the easy tail of the ranking. The 31%
  test-set 2023 rows likely contribute ≤5 bp of AUC variance to the
  total LB.
- **A per-year specialist won't help** unless the per-year sub-DGP
  has untapped information: 2023's AUC is already 0.95054, near
  ceiling; 2024 HARD (highest n × highest pos rate) is 0.8607 —
  the hardest to predict.
