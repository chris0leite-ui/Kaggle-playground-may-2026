## Devil's-advocate (Rule 26c) — could yekenot's 0.95273 be CV-fold-leaky?

The TE is `TargetEncoder(cv=5, smooth='auto')` fit on X_tr (train_fold +
orig_tr_fold) and transformed on X_val. sklearn's TargetEncoder cv=5
internally cross-fits on X_tr to avoid TE-leakage within fit; transform
on X_val uses the full-X_tr-fit map (no val-row labels). This is the
canonical CV-TE pattern; it should NOT leak.

So yekenot's 0.95273 is most likely genuine. Full-recipe replication is
a real +5-15 bp standalone-OOF lift opportunity (vs our default
realmlp 0.94582), with unknown K=22 ADD/SWAP transfer.
