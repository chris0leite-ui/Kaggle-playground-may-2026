# Day-9c — Factorization Machine baseline

> Tier-1 idea 2 from the post-d9b "what to test next" plan: build a
> Factorization Machine (FM) as a structurally different model class.
> Hypothesis: an FM auto-learns low-rank cross-feature interactions
> that the LR-with-hashed-2way (R14 ladder) had to bake in by hand.
> Builders `scripts/d9c_fm.py` + `scripts/d9c_kn_stack.py`.

## Mechanism

Factorization Machine model:
ŷ = w₀ + Σᵢ wᵢxᵢ + Σᵢ<ⱼ ⟨vᵢ, vⱼ⟩ xᵢxⱼ
where each feature i has a low-rank embedding vᵢ ∈ R^k. The pairwise
sum is computed in O(kn) per row via the identity
Σᵢ<ⱼ ⟨vᵢ, vⱼ⟩ xᵢxⱼ = ½ Σf [(Σᵢ vᵢ,f xᵢ)² − Σᵢ vᵢ,f² xᵢ²].

PyTorch CPU implementation (torch 2.11). Sparse embedding tables
(2¹⁸ buckets × k=8); SparseAdam for embeddings + Adam for the bias.
6 epochs, batch 8192, lr=0.05, BCEWithLogitsLoss.

## Features (main effects only)

Same 8 fields as R14_L2 (the d9b sweet spot) — *no* hand-engineered
interactions; FM learns them itself:

  D, C, R, Y, S, T_q5, Rp_q5, P_q5

(Driver, Compound, Race, Year, Stint-clip-5, plus 5-quintile bins of
TyreLife, RaceProgress, Position. Edges fitted on tr_idx only.)

## Results — single base

| Quantity | FM (d9c) | R14_L3 (d9b best) | R14_L0 (d9 R14) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| std OOF | **0.92069** | 0.91626 | 0.79377 |
| ρ vs PRIMARY test | **0.89858** | 0.87487 | 0.44358 |
| min-meta OOF | 0.95067 | 0.95065 | 0.95063 |
| Δ PRIMARY (bp) | **+0.18** | +0.01 | −0.02 |
| Verdict | **PASS ✓ (18× R14_L3 lift)** | PASS ✓ | FAIL |
| Wall (5-fold) | 56s | ~22 min | ~75s |

**FM passes the gate at +0.18bp** — same magnitude as the entire d9b
K=20 swap-L4 stack (which combined 4 d9 bases). And it does so with
lower ρ vs PRIMARY than R14_L3 (more diverse) **and** higher
standalone OOF (more informative). Both axes simultaneously — the
strict definition of a strictly-better base.

## Results — K=N stack

PRIMARY-keep = 14 M5q bases + 2 least-redundant rule_residuals
(rule_driver_compound, rule_year_race). d9-3 = R6, R10, R7.

| Stack | K | Δ PRIMARY OOF | ρ vs PRIMARY | pred-LB Δ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Sa K=21 (PRIMARY-keep + d9-3 + R14_L4 + FM) | 21 | +0.41bp | 0.99973 | +0.41 |
| Sb K=18 (PRIMARY-keep + R7 + FM) | 18 | +0.43bp | 0.99968 | +0.43 |
| Sc K=17 (PRIMARY-keep + FM solo) | 17 | +0.37bp | 0.99977 | +0.37 |
| **Sd K=20 swap (PRIMARY-keep + d9-3 + FM, drop R14)** | **20** | **+0.53bp** | **0.99973** | **+0.53** |

**Sd is the local optimum at +0.53bp predicted.** Above the +0.5bp
slot-worthy threshold for the first time since d3-endgame.

Sa (R14_L4 + FM) is **worse** than Sd (no R14) by 0.12bp — confirming
that R14 and FM occupy the same model-class slot. FM dominates;
including R14 in the same stack is double-counting.

Sb (just R7 + FM, no R6/R10/R14) hits +0.43bp at K=18, with R7
absorbing the role R7+R6+R10 played in Sd. Compact and almost as
strong; submission candidate if the K=20 ρ tightens.

## Why FM works where R14 ladder plateaued

R14 with hashed 2/3-way concatenations of categoricals operates in a
2¹⁸-dim sparse space; each interaction lives in its *own* bucket and
needs enough training rows to estimate a stable weight. Many low-
support buckets get noisy weights → diversity collapses (L4/L5 in
the ladder).

FM's k=8 embeddings make the *pairwise interaction* a function of
*8-dim feature vectors* rather than per-pair weights. This shares
statistical strength across all interactions and regularizes the
high-cardinality interaction surface to a low-rank manifold.
Concretely: the d9b R14 ladder needed L1 → L4 of feature engineering
to lift std OOF from 0.794 → 0.916; the FM hits 0.921 with **only the
8 main effects**.

The ρ vs PRIMARY (0.899 vs L3's 0.875) is also *lower* — suggesting
the FM's interaction structure is genuinely different from what the
GBDT bases extract, not a strict subset.

## Triage decision

**Sd K=20 swap with FM** is the slot-worthy candidate:
- Predicted Δ LB **+0.53bp**, above the +0.5bp slot threshold.
- ρ vs PRIMARY test **0.99973** — clears the HANDOVER 0.9995 gate
  with margin.
- Different model class than anything in PRIMARY's pool (linear
  GBDTs + RealMLP + lookups).

**Hold (do not submit yet) until PI sign-off:**
- F1.2 precedent: predicted +0.78bp, actual +2.1bp (≈3× upside).
- d9b L4 precedent: predicted +0.19bp, actual −0.01bp (tie).
- Sd's +0.53bp is in the "small but real" zone — probability of LB
  lift > 0 is ~70% by my prior, with expected magnitude 0.2–1.5bp.

If submitted and lifts, gap narrows from −3.9bp to ~−3.0bp; PRIMARY
rotates to Sd K=20 swap+FM.

## What to test next (post-d9c)

1. **FM with weight decay tuning + longer epochs**. The val-AUC
   curves plateaued by epoch 4 with mild oscillation; light WD or
   epoch-5 early stop might extract another 0.5–1.0bp std OOF, which
   plausibly pushes Sd toward +0.7bp.
2. **FM bagged across 3 seeds** (rank-average). Cheap (~3 min CPU);
   variance reduction adds another 0.1–0.3bp typical.
3. **FM with embed_dim sweep** (k ∈ {4, 8, 16}). 2 min each; might
   identify the rank sweet spot.
4. **External-data Pirelli pit-window scrape** (still highest absolute
   EV; orthogonal new signal — independent of FM gain).
