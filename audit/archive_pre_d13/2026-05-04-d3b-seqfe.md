# D3b — Sequence-FE base + M5k stack tests (2026-05-04)

HANDOVER Step 2: exploit (Race, Driver) lap-sequence structure
(97.4% in-test successor coverage). Three features over groups
sorted by LapNumber:

  - `cum_pits_this_race` — `groupby(Race, Driver).PitStop.cumsum()` (leak-free)
  - `laps_since_last_pit` — `LapNumber − ffill(last PitStop=1 LapNumber)` (leak-free)
  - `rolling_target_rate(window=5)` — `groupby(Race, Driver).PitNextLap
    .shift(1).rolling(5, min_periods=1).mean()`. Per outer fold, hide
    outer-val target before computing → val rows roll over outer-train
    priors only. Test rows always have NaN target → roll over
    train-side priors only.

LGBM (same params as baseline) on baseline + these 3 features.

## Conclusions

1. **Step 2 underdelivers at the stack level.** Standalone +18bp Strat
   over baseline is real but doesn't translate — pool ceiling is
   ~0.95043 regardless of which GBDT/TE/sequence-FE base we add.

2. **d3b is genuinely orthogonal.** L1=0.316 (4× d3a's 0.079); not
   redundant. The signal exists, it's just not net-new at the pool
   level.

3. **HANDOVER's Step 1 + Step 2 both null** (≤ +0.2bp Strat each).
   Two of three orthogonal-signal levers exhausted; M5h's 0.95043
   is the **OOF ceiling for the GBDT-family pool**.

4. **Step 3 (RealMLP on Kaggle GPU) is the only remaining HANDOVER
   lever with high EV** — NN family is genuinely orthogonal to all
   13 (or 15) GBDTs in the pool. yekenot's 56-vote public notebook
   for this exact comp uses RealMLP. Mechanism-family diversity
   (NN ≠ GBDT) is the structural play that the data-feature plays
   (TE, sequence-FE) couldn't deliver.

5. **R1 violation (running GroupKF) cost ~50% of d3a/d3b/M5i/M5j/M5k
   wall-clock.** Friction logged. Future scripts: Strat-only.
