## Tier-1 — CTGAN-aware exploits (NEW, motivated by F1 verdict)

The host is CTGAN-class. CTGAN's mechanism specifies three things to exploit
that we haven't directly used:

### G ⭐ — Mode-id attribution as categorical features
**Mechanism**: CTGAN uses Variational Gaussian Mixture (~10 modes) per
continuous feature. Mode-id IS the GAN's discrete latent. Synth's value =
`mode_mean[m] + mode_std[m] · residual` where m is the cond-generator's
choice. **We've never used mode-id as a feature.**

**Implementation**:
- Fit `BayesianGaussianMixture(n_components=10)` on each of the 7 KS-low
  features (TyreLife, Position, LapTime, CumDeg, RaceProgress,
  LapTime_Delta, LapNumber) on **orig only** (leakage-clean).
- For each synth row: argmax posterior → mode-id per feature.
- 7 new categorical features at 10 levels each.
- Add to LGBM downstream as `categorical_feature=` indices.

**Cost**: ~30 min CPU (BGMMs are fast on 99k rows).
**EV**: K=21+1 +1-3 bp (untried abstraction layer; mechanistically directly
maps CTGAN's discrete latent).
**Distinct from**: d16 (raw values), d18 (log-likelihoods), E2 (kNN), d15
leak_lookup (P(y\|value) at quantile bins, not VGM modes).
**Status**: 🔵 QUEUED — script `scripts/d18_g_mode_id_ctgan.py` to write.

### H — Mode-id × (Compound, Stint) trivariate orig class-rate lookup
**Mechanism**: combines G with d15 leak_lookup pattern. After mode-id
assignment, compute orig-empirical `P(y=1 | (Compound, Stint, mode_id_feat))`
for each KS-low feature → 7 lookup features per synth row.

**Cost**: ~10 min CPU after G's mode-ids exist.
**EV**: K=21+1 +0.5-2 bp; complements G (G uses tree splits on mode-id;
H uses orig-empirical class rates at the same axis).
**Status**: 🔵 QUEUED (depends on G).

### I — Mode-collapse / bias-factor features
**Mechanism**: GANs notoriously over-/under-sample modes. For each
(feature, mode-id), compute `bias_factor = synth_freq[mode] / orig_freq[mode]`.
Synth rows in over-sampled modes (bias_factor >> 1) are GAN artifacts;
their target rate likely drifts from orig's. **The first probe to target
the GAN's known failure mode directly.**

**Per-row features**: 7 bias-factors + composite `geom_mean(bias_factors)` =
8 features.
**Cost**: ~10 min CPU after G's mode-ids exist.
**EV**: K=21+1 +0.3-2 bp; novel mechanism, uncertain.
**Status**: 🔵 QUEUED (depends on G).

### J — Conditional-vector tuple lookup
**Mechanism**: CTGAN's cond vector likely included `(PitStop, Compound,
Stint, possibly Year/Race)`. Each synth row was generated with a specific
cond vector. **Look up that tuple's empirical class rate in orig directly.**

**Implementation**: `orig.groupby(['PitStop','Compound','Stint']).PitNextLap.mean()`;
synth row → look up its tuple → 1 feature. Plus `Year` extension → 1 more.
Plus tuple-frequency-in-orig as third.

**Cost**: ~5 min CPU.
**EV**: K=21+1 +0-1 bp (largely covered by existing rules; cheap to verify).
**Distinct from**: d6 rule features which are LGBM-derived, not direct
empirical lookups on the conditioning tuple.
**Status**: 🔵 QUEUED.

### K — Mode-id × chain_LL_q5 as Path-B cohort axis (replace failed E5 c1)
**Mechanism**: E5 c1 failed because chain_LL_q5 is a noisy continuous-derived
quintile. Mode-id is a clean discrete CTGAN latent. **Try mode-id of one
strong feature (e.g., TyreLife mode-id) × Compound as Path-B cohort axis
on K=22 + d18.**

**Implementation**: 5 (Compound) × 10 (TyreLife mode-id) = 50 cells. Path-B
hier-meta τ=20k.

**Cost**: ~10 min CPU (Path-B harness exists).
**EV**: untested but may finally fire 6-11.6× amp on a meta-arch axis;
Path-B-amp friction tag says base-adds don't fire amp, but META-ARCH
redesigns do.
**Status**: 🔵 QUEUED (depends on G).

### L — Per-(feature, mode-id) class-conditional KS table
**Mechanism**: pure diagnostic. For each (feature, mode-id), compute
KS(y=0 vs y=1) of the within-mode residual. Tells us "in which CTGAN
mode does the y-conditional shift the most?" Highest-KS modes are where
the GAN's class-conditional generator distorted reality the most.

**Cost**: ~5 min CPU.
**Output**: CSV table; not a base; informs L's interpretation of which
modes are most predictive.
**Status**: 🔵 QUEUED (depends on G).

### M — Within-mode residual discriminator (GAN-residual probe)
**Mechanism**: F1 proved disc on full row is ~0.99 AUC (host trivially
distinguishable). But what about disc on residuals ONLY (after mode-mean
subtracted)? Train classifier orig-residual vs synth-residual. If GAN's
residual distribution differs from orig's, the disc finds it. Per-row
disc score = "how fake-residual-like is this row."

**Cost**: ~30 min CPU.
**EV**: low-medium — F1 disc-as-base failed, but residual-only disc is
narrower and may extract incremental signal.
**Status**: 🔵 QUEUED (depends on G — needs mode-means).

### N — CTGAN replay → orig-LGBM transfer (alt to d16)
**Mechanism**: instead of training orig-LGBM on orig and predicting on
synth (d16's recipe), train **orig-LGBM on orig + CTGAN-replay
augmentation** to expand training data 2× with same DGP. The resulting
LGBM should be more robust to CTGAN-style corruptions when applied to
synth.

**Cost**: ~30 min CPU + the existing CTGAN replay artifact.
**EV**: speculative; depends on whether CTGAN's mode bias is similar
enough to host_synth's that augmentation transfers.
**Status**: 🔵 QUEUED (CTGAN replay exists from F1; restricted to 7 KS-low feats).
