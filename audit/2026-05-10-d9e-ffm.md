# Day-9e — Field-aware Factorization Machine (FFM)

> Tier-1 next-step from d9c FM landing at LB +3bp (NEW PRIMARY). FFM
> extends FM by giving each feature *F* embeddings (one per "target
> field") and using ⟨v_{i, f_j}, v_{j, f_i}⟩ for the i-j interaction.
> Hypothesis: per-field-pair specialization captures interaction
> structure that FM's single shared embedding cannot.
> Builders `scripts/d9e_ffm.py` (epochs=6) + `scripts/d9e_ffm_es.py`
> (epochs=2 early-stop). 281s + 241s wall = ~9 min total.

## Model

ŷ = w₀ + Σᵢ wᵢxᵢ + Σᵢ<ⱼ ⟨v_{i, f_j}, v_{j, f_i}⟩ xᵢxⱼ

PyTorch CPU. 2¹⁸ hash buckets × 8 fields × 4-dim ≈ 8.4M params (vs
FM's 2.1M). Same 8 main-effect features as d9c FM (D, C, R, Y, S +
TyreLife/RaceProgress/Position quintiles). SparseAdam + Adam(bias).

## Standalone — FFM is *worse* than FM

| Model | Std OOF | ρ vs PRIMARY | Min-meta Δ vs PRIMARY |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| FM (d9c, in PRIMARY pool) | **0.92069** | — | anchor |
| FFM-6ep (overfit) | 0.91525 | 0.877 | −0.06bp FAIL |
| FFM-2ep (early-stop) | 0.91869 | 0.886 | −0.05bp FAIL |

Per-fold val_AUC curves for FFM-6ep showed clear overfitting:

| Fold | epoch 0 | epoch 1 | epoch 2 | epoch 5 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 0 | **0.92062** | 0.92022 | 0.91965 | 0.91708 |
| 1 | 0.91661 | **0.91760** | 0.91631 | 0.91379 |
| 2 | 0.91684 | **0.91826** | 0.91742 | 0.91532 |
| 3 | 0.91766 | **0.91818** | 0.91652 | 0.91448 |
| 4 | 0.91804 | **0.91936** | 0.91822 | 0.91597 |

**Optimum = epoch 0 or 1** across all folds. Re-run at epochs=2 gave
std OOF 0.91869 — best the model can do but still 2bp below FM.

ρ vs PRIMARY is **higher** than expected (0.886 vs FM's 0.899 in d9c
test). The richer FFM parameterization produces *less-diverse*
predictions than the simpler FM, not more. The per-field-pair
specialization redundantly re-discovers the same interactions FM
captures with shared embeddings.

|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| K20 swap FM→FFM (6ep) | 20 | 0.95067 | −0.33bp | 0.99990 |
| K21 add FFM (6ep) | 21 | 0.95070 | +0.01bp | 0.99996 |
| K20 swap FM→FFM (2ep) | 20 | 0.95067 | −0.26bp | 0.99985 |
| K21 add FFM (2ep) | 21 | 0.95069 | −0.06bp | 0.99998 |

L1 ranking in K=21-add (FFM-2ep): FFM=0.318, FM_d9c=0.218 — *both*
demoted out of the top-12 (rule_driver_compound dominates at L1=0.997).
The LR meta finds no useful signal in either FM nor FFM at this
configuration; the GBDT-class bases carry the work.

## Why FFM does not help here

1. **Effective field count too small.** FFM gains over FM when there
   are many fields with unrelated semantics (e.g., FTRL-paper ad +
   user + publisher + creative). Our 8 fields (D, C, R, Y, S, T_q,
   Rp_q, P_q) already share statistical strength via FM's single
   embedding because they describe the same race-lap event.
2. **Parameter count → overfit.** 8.4M params for ~351k train rows ≈
   24 params per row. With only 1 active feature per field, the
   per-field-pair embeddings can memorize feature-pair training
   patterns. Overfitting kicks in by epoch 1.
3. **FM already saturates the interaction surface.** The d9c FM at
   k=8 captures ~all pairwise structure available; FFM's higher
   capacity adds variance, not bias.

## Triage decision

**HOLD** all d9e candidates. FFM does not beat FM in this setting.
The d9c PRIMARY (LB 0.95029) remains the local optimum within the
factorization-model family.

To extend the FM mechanism beyond d9c, the next-step probes need to
add **truly different inductive biases**, not richer parameterizations
of the same interaction surface:

1. **DeepFM-lite**: FM + 1-hidden-layer MLP head sharing embeddings.
   Adds non-linearity over the same low-rank space. ~1h CPU.
2. **Multi-FM with disjoint feature partitions**: 2 FMs with
   different feature subsets (e.g., one with D+C+S+T, another with
   R+Y+Rp+P), both stacked at K=22. Tests whether feature-set
   diversity gives a real second FM-class slot.
3. **FwFM (Field-weighted FM)**: a lightweight middle-ground between
   FM and FFM with 1 weight per field-pair instead of full per-pair
   embeddings. ~30 min CPU. May beat FFM by avoiding the overfit.
4. **External-data Pirelli pit-window scrape** (Tier-2 highest
   absolute EV, orthogonal new signal). Several hours engineering.
